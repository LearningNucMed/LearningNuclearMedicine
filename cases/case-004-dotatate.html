Case 004  ·  Neuroendocrine / DOTATATE

Metastatic Ileal NET with Hepatic Involvement

Staging and theranostic eligibility assessment in a patient with carcinoid syndrome

Clinical History
Patient Presentation

History

58-year-old woman presenting with a 2-year history of episodic flushing, secretory diarrhea, and right lower quadrant cramping.

Workup revealed elevated serum chromogranin A at 340 ng/mL (ref <93) and elevated 24-hour urine 5-HIAA. CT abdomen/pelvis demonstrated a 1.8 cm ileal mass with associated mesenteric desmoplastic reaction and multiple hepatic lesions, largest 2.3 cm in the right lobe.

CT-guided biopsy of a hepatic lesion confirmed well-differentiated neuroendocrine tumor, Grade 1 (Ki-67 <2%, mitotic rate <2/10 HPF). Referred to nuclear medicine for staging and theranostic assessment.

Clinical question: What is the extent of SSTR-expressing disease? Is this patient eligible for peptide receptor radionuclide therapy (PRRT) with Lu-177 DOTATATE?

Structured Findings

Primary Finding
Intensely DOTATATE-avid 1.8 cm mass in the distal ileum with associated mesenteric desmoplastic reaction and stellate mesenteric lymph nodes (up to 1.2 cm).
Secondary Findings
Three intensely avid hepatic lesions: segment VI (2.3 cm), segment V (1.1 cm), segment IVB (0.8 cm). No bone or pulmonary metastases. No brain involvement.

Expected high physiologic DOTATATE uptake in spleen, kidneys, liver, adrenals, pituitary, and uncinate process. No pathologic uptake in these regions.

Diagnosis
What Is the Answer?

Ready to see the diagnosis?

Make sure you've reviewed the images and formed your own impression first.

Diagnosis
Metastatic Well-Differentiated NET (G1)
SSTR-Positive — PRRT Candidate
Classification
WHO Grade 1 NET, ileal primary (Ki-67 <2%). ENETS / AJCC Stage IV (T3 N1 M1). ICD-10: C17.2 (ileum) with C78.7 (hepatic mets).
Why This Tracer
⁶⁸Ga-DOTATATE binds somatostatin receptor subtypes 2 and 5, which are overexpressed in well-differentiated NETs. High SUVmax reflects SSTR density — not glucose metabolism.
Theranostic Implication
All lesions demonstrate Krenning Score 4 (uptake > liver reference). Patient meets NETTER-1 criteria for Lu-177 DOTATATE (LUTATHERA) therapy.
Clinical Impact
This scan changes management: patient is referred for PRRT. The DOTATATE PET is the gatekeeper — no positive scan, no PRRT eligibility.
Teaching Points
Key Pearls
01
Use liver as your reference, never spleen. The spleen has uniformly intense physiologic DOTATATE uptake (SUVmax 60–80) and will make all lesions appear falsely low by comparison. Liver is the correct reference organ for Krenning scoring.
02
Krenning Score 4 is the PRRT threshold. Lesion uptake must exceed liver reference (Krenning ≥4) at all sites of disease for eligibility. A single Krenning 2 lesion can disqualify a patient — document each site individually.
03
Grade matters — high-grade NETs lose SSTR expression. G3 NETs (Ki-67 >20%) often downregulate somatostatin receptors and become DOTATATE-negative but FDG-avid. If DOTATATE scan is negative in a clinically obvious NET, consider FDG PET and review pathology grade.
04
Carcinoid syndrome ≠ DOTATATE-avid. Symptoms result from serotonin secretion — a functional marker — not SSTR expression. A symptomatic patient can still have low or absent DOTATATE uptake if the tumor is dedifferentiated.
05
The uncinate process of the pancreas is a normal variant trap. Physiologic DOTATATE uptake in the uncinate process can be intense and focal, mimicking a pancreatic NET primary. Correlation with CT morphology is essential — NETs typically show a discrete hypervascular mass.
